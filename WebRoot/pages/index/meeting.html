Call for Abstracts 

Over the past few years, we have seen a growing body of new trajectory data in understanding human activities and travel behaviors, such as vehicle GPS tracks, public transit records, mobile phone positioning data, as well as social network check-ins. The combination of this new wealth of information and the more conventional survey data could offer new insights to human dynamics.

Nevertheless, there remain debates on the usability and limitations of these new datasets. Individual datasets oftentimes present a biased and limited view of the reality. For example, the behavior of non-mobile phone users can hardly be captured by mobile phone data, and public transit records do not provide information about commuters using other modes of transportation. Therefore, we need an open discussion on the synthesis of multiple data sources in understanding travel dynamics.

This session will focus on the theories, methods, and applications of using new and conventional datasets to analyze the dynamics of human travel behavior. Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Big data analytics for travel dynamics
  • Statistical methods of analyzing tracing data
  • Integration of multiple datasets for trip purpose or activity inference
  • Integration of multiple datasets for travel demands analysis
  • Empirical comparison to understand travel behavior
  • Spatial-temporal dynamics of travel behavior
  • Multi-source data fusion methods
  • Data reliability analysis
